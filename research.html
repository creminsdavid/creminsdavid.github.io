THESIS! I am excited to share my senior thesis in Cognitive Science, "Music and Language: Exploring Evidence of Shared Neural Processing". For this work I was awarded the 2018 Fletcher Jones prize for the best senior thesis in Cognitive Science, as well as Distinction in the Senior Exercise from Pomona's Linguistics and Cognitive Science faculty. Please feel free to peruse the entire document and/or watch my presentation :)

I have also continued to collaborate with Dr. Martin Monti's research team at UCLA (with PhD student Micah Johnson and RA Aura Heredia) on another project investigating the neural links between music and language; check out the initial poster

***

I grew up steeped in the humanities, definitely for the better, but I think I have known from a young age that I intended to either explore the very large – stars, planets, and otherwise – or the very small – chemicals, neurons, and ‘otherwise’. While I am deeply fascinated with and care immensely about what lies in the middle (human affairs, history, the people around me), I am still a firm believer that society benefits greatly from basic science research of all varieties. Some of the most inspiring, compassionate, intelligent, and humanistic people I have met have been in laboratories, and I do not think this is a coincidence. I have the privilege to be in a position in life to dedicate myself to study of the brain and mind, and in light of this I am both grateful and determined.
I am eternally indebted to the friendship, guidance, connections, rigor, and passion of my Pomona College advisor and mentor Dr. Deborah Burke (pictured left in a reluctant selfie).

Research Skills: Python (Expyriment, PsychoPy, SciKit Learn), Matlab, statistics (R, Jasp, SPSS, Bayesian analysis), Freesurfer MRI analysis, Mango DTI analysis, TMS design, EPrime, CITI and NIH human subject safety certified, experimental scripting

Dr. Martin Monti's University of California, Los Angeles MontiLab

During the summer of 2015, after my first year of college, I was awarded funds by Pomona College's Summer Undergraduate Research Program (SURP) to work as a research assistant at UCLA. In Dr. Monti's lab, I learned a lot while living alone in a city for the first time, reading and reviewing articles, participating in lab meetings, helping PhD candidate Micah Johnson with the experimental design for his TMS (transcranial magnetic stimulation)-based thesis project, especially with the creation of tasks in the cognitive domains of music processing and action planning. While there, I was also recruited by researchers in Dr. Jesse Rissman's Memory Lab to help run participants for an fMRI study ( poster). I earned pocket money by participating in various imaging studies (see my brain at the top right!), and presented an overview of some of my work for the summer at the annual Pomona College Poster Fair.

Dr. Arturo Hernandez's University of Houston Laboratory for the Neural Bases of Bilingualism

During the summer of 2016, I was awarded another SURP to do a summer fellowship in Dr. Hernandez's lab under the supervision of postdoc Dr. Pilar Archila-Suerte. While self-teaching myself R (and making this website), my main responsibilities were to edit and analyze structural brain scans using primarily Freesurfer tools, editing and creating Matlab scripts for in-progress experiments, and designing/coding a cognitive control experiment for bilinguals and monolinguals (using Python/Expyriment). More details can be found in this poster.

Data Science Research Internship at the Argentine Fulbright Commission

During the fall semester of 2016, I worked 2 days per week at the Argentine Commission in charge of allocating Fulbright scholarships to students, professors, researchers, and artists hoping to study in the United States. Apart from other roles assisting in the office, I took upon myself the task of accumulating data from past scholarship years and analyzing it in order to understand the trends in applicants characteristics and success rates. Although in Spanish, the result of this research project can be found in this paper.
