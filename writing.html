I owe a great deal to my mom and dad, including their literary influence on my life. Having writer/teacher/English major parents (check out me to the left on my mom's campus, UC Santa Cruz, go Banana Slugs!) has shaped the way I think, talk, and interact with the world. Frankly, I sometimes feel I am turning my back on my most natural academic inclinations - Literature and History classes were always where I felt most comfortable growing up. However, if going to a small liberal arts college (and studying science there while working at Pomona's Writing Center.!) has taught me anything so far, it is that language, expression, and critical thinking permeate every facet of life. As long as I can speak, write, or sing, I'm sure I'll be happy.

I got to delve into creative writing again in my last semester of college, as well, via a class taught by one of my favorite authors, Jonathan Lethem, producing this piece :D

Excerpts and Speeches

Linked above are blog posts containing some excerpts covering nearly a decade of my life, selected from various journals, notebooks, events, and school assignments. The thoughts throughout reveal parts of myself and my life that I had forgotten about, or not thought about for many years. The writing is, to me at least, often very funny, sometimes sad, but always honest. Undertaking this project, I admired how surprisingly aware of life I seemed to be from a young age, and embarrassed to read about some of my hypocrisies at the same time. For better or worse, I had no embarrassment about putting down on paper what was truly important to me back then, and I hope this will give you too a glimpse into who I am.
